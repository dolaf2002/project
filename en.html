Razmik Karen Tandilyan was born on July 6, 2001, in the village of Haghpats in the Lori region of Armenia. From 2007 to 2019, he studied at Haghpat Secondary School. He loved Armenian history and Russian language. He was a favorite of the teachers and the joy of his classmates, as he was very funny, friendly, and trustworthy.

In 2019, after graduating from school, he entered the National Polytechnic University of Armenia. However, after four months of studying, he was drafted into the army on January 15, 2020. He served in Hadrut as a garrison soldier. He never complained. When asked by his mother, he would always respond: "Everything is fine here, we are all here."

After nine months of service, the 44-day war began. On the morning of September 27, they were called to the front lines. On October 4, he was transferred to a command post. According to his comrades, he fought with bravery worthy of a real hero. After continuous firing, his machine gun broke. He requested a new one.

On October 7, during his last conversation with his mother, he reassured her: "Don't worry, everything is calm here." He told his father: "Tell them not to send anyone from the village, I will fight for all of them." When his brother asked, "Have you seen any Turks?", he replied: "As many as you want." When his brother asked, "Are you standing or lying?", Razmik replied laughing, "Standing, I’m putting them down!"

On October 9, Razmik died while securing the command post from artillery fire.

A memorial fountain was built in the yard of his school in Haghpat in his memory, and a memorial stone was placed along the road leading to the village. In the village, a cross was erected in his memory and in honor of all the fallen heroes.

One of his comrades in arms, speaking about him, always says, "He was a guy with a heart of gold"... His father often remembers one of the conversations with his son, when he said he didn’t want to go to the army, to which Razmik replied, "If I don't go, who will go?"

After his death, Razmik was posthumously awarded the "Combat Service" medal by the President of Nagorno-Karabakh, the "Gratitude" medal by the student council of NPUA, and the "Hovhannes Tumanyan" and "Combat Cross" medals by the First Battalion public organization.

During his school years, Razmik was a poet. He wrote over twenty poems. With the support of his alma mater, a book of his poems titled "Poetic Lines from the Depths of the Heart" was published.
